• Heart Rate Problems

  • Key Questions to take note: Description of the sensation of the heart rate, what was patient doing prior to having the palpitation problem(activities), associated symptoms, any medications taken, duration of the sensation, any relieved by medication

    Other Protocols to consider: Congestive Heart Failure, Chest Pain, Dizziness, Breathing Problem

    Question A: Are any of the complaints present?

    • Is the heart rate > 150 beats per miunte (if there is a blood pressure set with pulse rate where patient can read or patient is able to calculate from the palpation of radial pulse based on one minute)
    • Presisted rapid heart rate of > 150 beats per minute for >30 minutes with increase fatigue
    • Chest, neck, jaw or arm pain or discomfort
    • Diffculty in breathing
    • Skin cool and moist or hot and dry
    • Face and lips blue or very pale
    • Altered mental status/Fainting
    • Repeated shocks with internao defibrillator in place(if ACID in place)

    IF YES,

    Instruct care giver to patient to ACTIVATE 995 Ambulance for help

    CareHub Team to activate 995 Ambulance on behalf if:

    • Patient sounded in distress
    • Drowsy
    • Altered mental status

    Ensure patient GCS is 15 and alert

    IF NO,

    Go to Question B

    Question B: Are any of the complaints present?

    • Presistent rapid heart rate of >150 beats per minute for >30 minutes but not increasing fatigue
    • Light-headedness, faintness or dizziness
    • Presistent rapid heart rate and history of thyroid disease or heart disease
    • Slow heart rate and extremely fatigue or frequent episodes of slow heart beat(

    IF YES,

    Consult nearest GP immediately

    Check if medications are taken(if not taken yet)

    Ask patient to sit down. If patient knows the vasovagal stimulation method(CAUTIONS! Only for Supravetricular Tachycardia. If patient is tagut by physicians or nurses and ONLY has done it before!) Instruct patient to do so, if not, seek medical attention by going to the nearest GP

    Check if patient is taking beta blockers ro other cardiac medications. Is patient becoming ore lethargic and restless(please proceed back to A for immediate attention)

    Consider Escalation Protocol

    Consider bringing forward TCU appointment

    Instruct care giver or patient to ACTIVATE 995 Ambulance for help or proceed to seek help at the hospital("Do not drive alone")

    CareHub Team to activate 995 Ambulance on behalf if:

    • Patient sounded in distress
    • Drowsy
    • Altered mental status

    IF NO,

    Go to Question C

    Question C: Are any of the complaints present?

    • Frequent episodes of a rapid heart rate
    • History of prior treatment for rapid heart rate
    • Recent ingestion of duretics, diet pills, decongestatns, colde remedies, Beta blockers, thyroid medication, a new medication
    • History of bronchodilator use and new prescriptions or increase in dose
    • Excessive caffeine, tobacoo, alcohol, TCM or herbals used
    • Diffculty sleeping or presistent fatigue
    • Increase in stress
    • Exercise
    • Unexplained weight gain, fatigue and feeling cold
    • Fever(Temperature > 37.5 C)

     

    IF YES,

    Consult nearest GP within 24 hours or nearest Primary Health Care

    Check if medications are taken(if not taken yet)

    IF NO,

    Follow Home Care Instructions

    Home Care Instructions

    • Vasovagal method only if patient is trained by physician or nurses (Caution! Method is only for Supraventricular Tachycardia).
    • Take deep breaths: hold and pinch nostril closed. Gently try to exhale through the nose; take deep breath and bear down as if trying to pass motion(hold for a few seconds and exhale) Patient will feel light headedness. Do not attempt again if feeling fainting spell/nausea or vomiting
    • Take cold shower and let the cold water splase on the face and head
    • Try to rest and relax
    • Avoid caffeine and alcohol
